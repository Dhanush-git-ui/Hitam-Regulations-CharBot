any exceptions.

regulations.

(i) Generate cumulative attendance records every Saturday using the ERP data, under the 
supervision of Head Operations, and share that with the concemed class teachers, 
coordinators, HODs and leadership team. 
(ii) The reports will also contain the projected attendance of the students. 
(iii) Make calls to the parents and document the conversations as per the identified week-on- 
week action plan, arrange for PTMs and maintain a record of declarations signed by the 
parents 
(iv) In case a parent is not available on phone and / or doesn't come for the PTM and / or 
doesn't sign a declaration, the Head Operations will arrange for a video conference with

(v) In case the parent is not accessible over phone or video conference, the Head Operations

(vi) In case the courier / registered post is returned to origin, a physical home visit will be

(c) Mentor: 
(i) Interact with students having low attendance by coordinating with the respective subject

(i) Document the mentoring sessions in the mentor records 
(i) Refer the students who need support to the wellness counselor 
(i) Speak to the parents, and create awareness about the attendance of the student 
(ii) Inform the class teachers regarding parents who need to sign an undertaking, and 
coordinate for the interaction 
(d) Class Teacher: 
(i) Monitor if the mentors called the parents and updated the mentor records 
(i) Coordinate with Head Operations to arrange for declaration from parents of students with 
low attendance, as per the identified weekly action plan, and plan for PTMs 
(e) Department Coordinator: 
(i) Review the program office attendance reports against the subject faculty reports 
(ii) Coordinate with mentors and class teachers for communication with parents regarding 
attendance 
(iii) Monitor the class teachers for collecting undertaking from parents 
(iv)Act as a liaison between program office, faculty and parents, facilitating effective 
communication 
(f) Head of the Department (HOD): 
(i) Handle faculty escalations related to student attendance. 
(i) Assess and address attendance issues that cannot be resolved by the faculty. 
(ii) Collaborate with the faculty to develop strategies for improving attendance.
